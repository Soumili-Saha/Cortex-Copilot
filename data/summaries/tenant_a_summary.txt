[TENANT A - DATASET OVERVIEW]
This knowledge base describes the electrical power-quality and energy dataset for Tenant A. Every figure below belongs to Tenant A only. The dataset contains 5,923 readings logged at roughly 10-minute intervals (most common interval 00:10:02). Monitoring began on 2026-05-19 16:35:19 and ended on 2026-07-04 08:27:57, spanning about 45 days and 16 hours. After cleaning, no missing values remain. The three phases are labelled R (Red), Y (Yellow), and B (Blue).

[TENANT A - ENERGY CONSUMPTION AND DEMAND]
Over the ~45-day period, Tenant A consumed 2,438,003.61 kWh of total active energy and 2,611,868.35 kVAh of apparent energy. The load factor was 38.79%, meaning average load sits well below peak - Tenant A has sharp, variable peaks rather than a flat load, which drives demand charges.

[TENANT A - VOLTAGE QUALITY]

Using a nominal of 421.18 V, Tenant A had 10 voltage sag events (below 90% of nominal) and 0 swell events (above 110% of nominal). [TENANT A - POWER FACTOR]
The true power factor magnitude is around 0.95 but was recorded with a negative sign (mean -0.15, median -0.95). This points to a wiring issue - a reverse-connected current transformer (CT) or reverse power flow - rather than genuinely poor efficiency. The recorded power factor fell below 0.85 for 58.0% of intervals (3,433 readings). If this is a real low-PF condition it would attract power-factor penalties on the bill, but the negative sign should be investigated first by checking CT polarity.

[TENANT A - HARMONIC DISTORTION (THD)]
Harmonic distortion is a major concern for Tenant A. Voltage THD exceeded the IEEE-519 5% limit on 27.17% of readings for R phase (max 35.24%), 30.05% for Y phase (max 25.74%), and 23.65% for B phase (max 25.13%). Current THD is far worse, exceeding the 8% limit on 75.08% of readings for R phase (max 370.74%), 83.05% for Y phase (max 400.55%), and 89.99% for B phase (max 425.57%). These very high current-harmonic levels indicate heavy non-linear loads such as variable-frequency drives or rectifiers.

[TENANT A - TRANSFORMER STRESS (K-FACTOR)]
K-Factor measures transformer overheating risk from harmonics; values above 4.0 indicate stress. For Tenant A, K_Factor_I_R averaged 2.59 (max 10.35) and exceeded 4.0 for 20.48% of the time; K_Factor_I_Y averaged 2.66 (max 10.43), above 4.0 for 18.72%; K_Factor_I_B averaged 2.96 (max 10.18), above 4.0 for 28.65%. The transformer serving Tenant A is periodically overstressed and may need de-rating or harmonic filtering.

[TENANT A - GRID FREQUENCY]
Grid frequency averaged 49.95 Hz (median 50.00 Hz), within the normal Indian 50 Hz band, staying below 50.09 Hz for 95% of readings and peaking at 50.31 Hz. Occasional 0.00 Hz readings correspond to metering dropouts, not real grid events.

[TENANT A - ANOMALIES (ISOLATION FOREST)]
An Isolation Forest with 2% contamination flagged 119 abnormal intervals for Tenant A out of 5,923 readings. During anomalies, average total power rose to 863,513.87 W versus 542,942.87 W normally, and total current rose to 1,273.41 A versus 775.63 A. Voltage THD roughly doubled during anomalies (R 7.14% vs 3.14%, Y 7.13% vs 3.23%, B 6.88% vs 3.16%) and current THD climbed (I_R 54.57% vs 17.54%, I_B 51.90% vs 20.52%). Anomalies therefore correspond to high-demand, high-distortion periods: the system is most stressed when load is heaviest.

[TENANT A - DATA QUALITY CAVEATS]
Three known data issues affect Tenant A and should be mentioned when relevant. First, the Num_Interruptions register reports an impossible cumulative total (about 1.886 billion), a hardware counter overflow, so the true interruption count is unknown and this number must not be quoted literally. Second, the true power factor carries a persistent negative sign (median -0.95), most likely a reverse-connected CT or reverse power flow, so the PF sign should be verified before drawing efficiency conclusions. Third, some THD readings are negative (physically impossible) and were treated as sensor errors. Energy totals were computed from positive differences of the cumulative registers to ignore meter resets and rollovers.
